A personal recovery dashboard for Apple Watch.

Pulse turns the data your Apple Watch already collects into Whoop style recovery, strain, and sleep scores, computed entirely on your iPhone.

  • Scores you against your own rolling HRV and resting heart rate baselines.
  • Strain from time in heart rate zones, on a 0 to 21 logarithmic scale.
  • Runs on your device. No account, no server, nothing leaves the phone.

What it measures

Three scores, from one wrist.

Each score is a pure function of your Health data: deterministic, explainable, and calibrated against your own history rather than a population average.

Recovery

80%

Last night's HRV and resting heart rate, standardized against your rolling 14 and 28 day baselines and blended with sleep. Green when you're ready, red when you're run down.

Strain

12.5of 21

Cardiovascular load accumulated from time in heart rate zones across workouts and daily activity, on a logarithmic scale where each additional point is harder to earn.

Sleep

8:11hr

Hours slept against a personal need that grows with the prior day's strain and any sleep debt built up over the week, plus the full stage breakdown.

How it's built

One codebase, no dependencies.

Pulse reads the last 180 days from HealthKit (HRV, resting and workout heart rate, sleep stages, energy, VO₂ max) and assembles them into a single dataset that the scoring engine turns into daily scorecards.

The engine is pure Swift. Recovery comes from HRV and resting heart rate standardized against rolling baselines, strain from time in heart rate zones on a saturating 0 to 21 curve, and sleep from a need that responds to strain and debt. Every value is reproducible from its inputs.

The interface follows a deliberate warm studio design system: a paper canvas, a single typeface with weight reserved for data, and status color used only where a metric genuinely carries a status.
