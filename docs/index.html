A personal recovery dashboard for Apple Watch.

It reads what your Apple Watch already records and works out recovery, strain, and sleep scores on the phone, the way WHOOP does with its own band.

  • The scores are relative to your own HRV and resting heart rate history, not a general population.
  • Strain is built from time in each heart rate zone, on a 0 to 21 scale.
  • Everything runs on the phone. There is no account and no server.

What it measures

The three scores

Each one is worked out from your Health data and measured against your own recent history.

Recovery

80%

Last night's HRV and resting heart rate, set against your 14 and 28 day baselines and combined with how you slept. Higher means more rested.

Strain

12.5of 21

Time spent in each heart rate zone, across workouts and ordinary movement, on a 0 to 21 scale that counts harder efforts for more.

Sleep

8:11hr

Hours slept against how much you needed that night, which rises after hard days and when you are behind on sleep. The stage breakdown is there too.

Details

How it's built

Pulse reads the last 180 days from HealthKit (HRV, resting and workout heart rate, sleep stages, energy, and VO₂ max) and scores each day from that.

The scoring is plain Swift with no outside libraries. Each score is worked out straight from the numbers, so it is always possible to see why a given day came out the way it did.

The design is deliberately quiet. A warm paper background, one typeface, and color only where a number carries a status. I wanted it to feel closer to a measuring instrument than a fitness app.
